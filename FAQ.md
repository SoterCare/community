# ❓ Community FAQ

**Q: Who can join SoterCare?**
Anyone! We're student-led, but students, graduates, professionals, and enthusiasts are all welcome. There's no application — start by joining an event or making a contribution.

**Q: Do I need to know how to code?**
No. We need designers, writers, organizers, photographers, and speakers just as much as developers. See [Ways to Contribute](CONTRIBUTING.md#ways-to-contribute).

**Q: I've never contributed to open source. Where do I start?**
The [First Contribution Guide](guides/first-contribution.md) walks you through it step by step, and issues labeled [`good first issue`](https://github.com/search?q=org%3ASoterCare+label%3A%22good+first+issue%22+state%3Aopen&type=issues) are reserved for newcomers.

**Q: Is SoterCare free?**
Yes. All community events, workshops, and programs are free for students.

**Q: How do I stay updated about events?**
Follow us on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/sotercare) and [Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/sotercare_/), and watch this repository (Watch → Custom → Discussions) for announcements.

**Q: Can my community/company partner with SoterCare?**
We'd love to talk — reach out via [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/sotercare) or open a [Community Suggestion](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose).

**Q: Can I speak at a SoterCare event?**
Yes! Open a [Workshop Proposal](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose) with your topic, and read the [Speaker Checklist](templates/speaker-checklist.md).

**Q: How do I become a mentor / volunteer / organizer?**
Each path has a guide: [Mentor](guides/become-a-mentor.md), [Volunteer](guides/volunteer-guide.md), and organizer roles grow out of consistent volunteering — see [Governance](GOVERNANCE.md).

**Q: Where does the product code live?**
In the org's other repositories: [github.com/SoterCare](https://github.com/SoterCare). This repository is the community's home — events, guides, and resources.

**Q: Who do I contact about a Code of Conduct concern?**
See [enforcement](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md#enforcement) — reports are confidential.
