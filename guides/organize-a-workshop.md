# 🎓 How to Organize a Workshop

The playbook for running a SoterCare workshop — from idea to published report. Our workshops (AI, Cloud, Blockchain, bootcamps) follow this pattern.

## 1. Shape the idea

Answer these before proposing:

- **Topic & takeaway:** what will attendees *be able to do* afterwards? ("Deploy a smart contract on Algorand testnet" — not "learn about blockchain")
- **Audience & level:** who is it for, and what must they already know?
- **Format:** hands-on beats lecture. Aim for 60% doing, 40% listening.
- **Instructor:** you, another member, or an external speaker?

## 2. Propose it

Open a [Workshop Proposal issue](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose). The core team will help with venue, promotion, and pairing you with an experienced organizer if it's your first time.

## 3. Plan (3–4 weeks out)

- Follow the [Event Checklist](../templates/event-checklist.md) — it covers registration, promotion, and logistics
- Collect [Speaker Information](../templates/speaker-information.md) from every instructor
- **Prepare the hands-on path:** exercises tested on a fresh machine, prerequisites written down, install instructions sent to attendees *before* the day (installation eats workshops alive)
- Prepare a "catch-up" plan: a checkpoint repo or snippets so someone who falls behind can jump back in

## 4. Structure that works

For a 2-hour hands-on workshop:

| Time | Segment |
| --- | --- |
| 0:00–0:10 | Welcome, Code of Conduct, what we'll build |
| 0:10–0:30 | Concepts — just enough to start |
| 0:30–1:30 | Guided hands-on, with checkpoints every ~15 min |
| 1:30–1:50 | Challenge / extension exercise |
| 1:50–2:00 | Wrap-up, resources, feedback form, what's next |

Volunteers roam during hands-on segments — one helper per ~8 attendees.

## 5. On the day

Run the [Event Checklist](../templates/event-checklist.md) day-of section. Golden rules: start the *content* on time, never debug one person's laptop for 10 minutes while the room waits (that's what roaming helpers are for), and end with a concrete "here's what to do next".

## 6. Afterwards

- [Workshop Report](../templates/workshop-report.md) within a week
- Slides to [`slides/`](../slides/), code to the workshop folder, photos to [`photos/`](../photos/)
- Thank instructors and volunteers publicly; add attendees' follow-up questions to Discussions

First-time organizer? You won't be alone — the [Organizer Checklist](../templates/organizer-checklist.md) and a co-organizer have your back.
