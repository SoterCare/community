# 🧑‍✈️ Organizer Guide

Everything you need to lead a SoterCare event, whatever its type. This is the umbrella guide; the playbooks below go deep on specific formats.

## The organizer's job

As organizer, you're accountable for the event happening well. You don't do everything yourself — you make sure everything has an owner and nothing falls through the cracks.

## The universal flow

1. **Propose** — open an [Event Proposal](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose); get it approved
2. **Plan** — work the [Event Checklist](../templates/event-checklist.md) from 4 weeks out
3. **Build the team** — assign every role to a *name*; pair new volunteers with experienced ones ([Volunteer Guide](volunteer-guide.md))
4. **Confirm speakers** — collect [Speaker Information](../templates/speaker-information.md); share the [Speaker Checklist](../templates/speaker-checklist.md)
5. **Promote** — announce, then remind (LinkedIn, Instagram, campus channels)
6. **Run it** — follow the [Organizer Checklist](../templates/organizer-checklist.md) on the day
7. **Report & recognize** — publish an [event page](../events/) within a week; thank and [recognize](../docs/recognition.md) everyone

## Your responsibilities as lead

- A co-lead can cover for you if you're unavailable
- A plan for the three classic failures: speaker cancels, internet dies, half the registrants don't show
- Code of Conduct contact named in opening remarks
- Honest lessons captured in the event report, so the next organizer starts ahead of where you did

## First time?

You won't be alone. Every organizer here started by volunteering at someone else's event, and you'll have a co-organizer and the [checklists](../templates/) backing you up.
