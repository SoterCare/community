# 🚀 How to Organize a Hackathon

Hackathons are our highest-effort, highest-impact events — VisioNEX reached 500+ school students from 40+ schools. This guide captures how we run them. A hackathon needs a real organizing team and 6–8 weeks of lead time; don't attempt one solo.

## 1. Define the shape (8+ weeks out)

- **Theme & tracks:** healthcare tech is our home turf, but pick what serves the audience
- **Audience:** university students? school students (like VisioNEX)? mixed? This changes *everything* — difficulty, duration, supervision, prizes
- **Duration & format:** 8-hour sprint / 24-48h classic / multi-week online
- **Success metric:** decide it now (participants? projects submitted? first-time hackers?)

Open an [Event Proposal issue](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose) with these answers.

## 2. Build the team

Minimum viable organizing team, each role owned by a name:

| Role | Owns |
| --- | --- |
| Lead | The whole thing — see [Organizer Checklist](../templates/organizer-checklist.md) |
| Logistics | Venue, food, power, internet, badges |
| Participants | Registration, communication, teams formation |
| Judging | Judges, criteria, rubric, awards |
| Partnerships | Sponsors, prizes, partner communities |
| Content | Opening/closing ceremony, workshops during the hack, MC |

## 3. Secure the essentials (6 weeks out)

- Venue with reliable internet and enough power outlets (count them — seriously)
- Sponsors/partners for prizes and food — our [partners](../README.md#-community-partners) are a starting point
- Judges: 3–5, with a mix of technical and domain expertise
- A clear, published **judging rubric** (innovation / execution / impact / presentation is a solid default)

## 4. Participant experience

- Registration with team-or-solo option; run team formation for solos at kickoff
- Rules published in advance: team size, what can be pre-built (usually: nothing but ideas), submission format, IP stays with teams
- Submission via a form or platform — require repo link + 2-minute demo video; demos die, videos don't
- Mentors circulating during the hack (this is where our [mentors](become-a-mentor.md) shine)
- For school students: shorter duration, more structure, guardian communication, and stricter safeguarding — brief every volunteer

## 5. The day(s)

- Kickoff: theme, rules, rubric, Code of Conduct, where to get help
- Checkpoints every few hours: announcements, energy, mentor rotations
- Hard submission deadline — enforce it kindly but absolutely
- Judging: shortlist from submissions, live demos/pitches from finalists
- Closing: celebrate *everyone*, not just winners; every first-time hacker finished something

## 6. Afterwards

- [Hackathon Summary](../templates/hackathon-summary.md) within a week — winners, numbers, lessons
- Photos, project links, and social recap published
- Personal thank-yous to judges, sponsors, mentors, volunteers
- Retro with the organizing team; lessons go in the summary, not in someone's memory
