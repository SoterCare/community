# 🙋 Volunteer Guide

Volunteers make every SoterCare event possible — and volunteering is the path into organizing, mentoring, and community leadership. This is both the "how to become a volunteer" guide and the handbook for volunteering well.

## Becoming a volunteer

1. Read this guide and the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
2. Watch for volunteer calls before events (Discussions + social media), or open a [Community Suggestion issue](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose) saying you'd like to volunteer and what interests you
3. You'll be added to the volunteer pool and pinged when events need hands

No experience needed. Every organizer in this community started as a volunteer at someone else's event.

## Volunteer roles at events

| Role | What you do |
| --- | --- |
| Registration desk | Welcome attendees, check them in, set the tone |
| Roaming helper | Support attendees during hands-on sessions |
| Tech support | Projector, mics, streaming, the speaker's HDMI adapter crisis |
| Photographer | Capture the event (see [photo guidelines](../photos/README.md)) |
| Timekeeper | Keep sessions on schedule with friendly signals |
| Social media | Live coverage, recap material |

## What you can expect from us

- A clear role and briefing before the event — never "just show up and figure it out"
- A named organizer to escalate anything to
- Public recognition in event reports and [Contributor Recognition](../docs/recognition.md)
- A genuine path forward: volunteers → organizers → core team (see [Governance](../GOVERNANCE.md))

## What we expect from you

- Show up when you commit, or give the organizer early notice — coverage is planned around you
- Be the friendliest person in the room, especially to whoever looks lost
- Know the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md) and who the event's contact person is
- Flag problems early; small issues caught at the registration desk stay small

## Beyond events

Volunteering isn't only event-day work: maintaining this repo, curating [resources](../resources/), designing graphics for [`assets/`](../assets/), and writing event recaps all count — and all show up in your contribution history.
