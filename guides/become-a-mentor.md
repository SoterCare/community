# 🧑‍🏫 Become a Mentor

Mentors are how SoterCare scales learning: experienced members guiding newcomers through first contributions, projects, and hackathons. Mentoring is also the fastest way to deepen your own understanding — nothing tests knowledge like explaining it.

## Who can mentor?

You don't need to be an expert. You need to be **one step ahead** of the person you're helping, and generous with your time. If you've merged a few PRs, shipped a project, or run a workshop segment, you can mentor someone who hasn't.

## What mentors do

- **First-contribution mentoring:** guide a newcomer through the [first contribution flow](first-contribution.md) — from fork to merged PR
- **Project mentoring:** pair with members on SoterCare projects; review code with teaching in mind
- **Hackathon mentoring:** circulate during hackathons, unblock teams without building for them
- **Workshop support:** be a roaming helper at hands-on sessions

## What mentors don't do

- Do the work for the mentee (unblocking ≠ solving)
- Commit to more hours than they have — 1–2 hours a week is a real contribution
- Gatekeep: "that's a basic question" is not in our vocabulary

## How to become one

1. Read this guide and the [Code of Conduct](../CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
2. Open a [Community Suggestion issue](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/issues/new/choose) titled "Mentor application: your-name" with: your areas (AI / Cloud / IoT / Web3 / Web / Mobile / open source basics), your availability, and a link to something you've done (PRs, project, talk)
3. A core team member will chat with you and add you to the mentor pool
4. You'll be matched with mentees or events based on your areas

Mentors are listed in our [Contributor Recognition](../docs/recognition.md) and get priority consideration for organizer roles.

## Getting a mentor

Looking for mentorship instead? Open a [Discussion](https://github.com/SoterCare/community/discussions) describing what you're trying to learn or comment on the issue you're working on — a mentor will pick it up.

## Mentoring well: quick principles

- Ask questions before giving answers ("what have you tried?")
- Prefer pointing at docs over reciting them — teach the finding, not just the fact
- Celebrate small wins loudly; struggle is invisible, so name the progress you see
- If a mentee goes quiet, one friendly nudge — then let it be; life happens
